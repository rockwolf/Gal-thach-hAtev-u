\section{Menghavan 9: Gwepr\'{a}i\'{e}}
(\textit{Lesson 9: Prepositions})\\
%\noindent In the nineth lesson you will learn about prepositions. A preposition is a word that provides information about the location, situation or position of something.
%\noindent <strong>Prepositions</strong>
%\noindent All prepositions in modern Gaulish cause initial consonant mutation on the word that follows them.
%\noindent esi: is
%\noindent ép: horse
%\noindent anel: underneath
%\noindent pren: tree
%\noindent &gt; esi ép anel <strong>b</strong>ren: there is a horse underneath a tree (litt. “a horse is underneath a tree”)
%\noindent sédhi: sit
%\noindent gwir: man
%\noindent ur: against
%\noindent carch: rock
%\noindent &gt; sédha gwir ur <strong>g</strong>arch: a man sits against a rock
%\noindent esi: is
%\noindent gwolth: hair
%\noindent en: in
%\noindent iuth: soup
%\noindent &gt; esi gwolth en <strong>ch</strong>’iuth: there is a hair in a soup
%\noindent esi: am
%\noindent mi: I
%\noindent e: from
%\noindent tóth: people
%\noindent ríu: free
%\noindent &gt; esi mi e <strong>d</strong>óth ríu: I am from a free people
%\noindent áia: go
%\noindent í: she
%\noindent a: to
%\noindent tráith: beach
%\noindent &gt; áia í a <strong>d</strong>ráith: she goes to a beach
%\noindent Prepositions do not cause mutations on the article “in” or on possessive pronouns
%\noindent &gt; esi ép gwó <strong>i</strong>n pren
%\noindent &gt; esi gwolth en <strong>m</strong>ó ch’iuth: there is a hair in my soup
%\noindent <strong>With personal pronouns</strong>
%\noindent Prepositions fuse with personal pronouns:
%\noindent can: with
%\noindent mi: I
%\noindent &gt; canim: with me
%\noindent gwer: on
%\noindent ti: you
%\noindent &gt; gwerith: on you
%\noindent nes: near
%\noindent ni: we
%\noindent &gt; nesin: near us
%\noindent <strong>Patterns</strong>
%\noindent Prepositions fuse with personal pronouns according to regular patterns. There are four different categories for these patterns.
%<ol>
%<li>Prepositions ending in consonants</li>
%</ol>
%\noindent These are:
%\noindent can, ar, ern, ur, cin, ós, gwer, en, tar, am, ér, échan, enther, uchel, anel
%\noindent (with, before, behind, against, after, on, in, through, about, around, without, between, above, below)
%\noindent These take the following endings:
%\noindent -im, -ith, -é, -í, -in, -sú, -ís
%\noindent canim: with me
%\noindent canith: with you
%\noindent cané: with him
%\noindent caní: with her
%\noindent canin: with us
%\noindent cansú: with you (pl.)
%\noindent canís: with them
%\noindent The emphasis in these forms falls on the last syllable:
%\noindent can’<strong>i</strong>m: with <em>me</em>
%<ol start="2">
%<li>Prepositions ending in vowels</li>
%</ol>
%\noindent These are:
%\noindent ri, di, tré, co, éithra, anó, echó (for, off, across, than/as, beyond, inside, outside)
%\noindent These take the following endings:
%\noindent -em, -eth, -ché, -chí, -en, -sú, -chís
%\noindent riem: for me
%\noindent rieth: for you
%\noindent riché: for him
%\noindent richí: for her
%\noindent rien: for us
%\noindent risú: for you (pl.)
%\noindent richís: for them
%\noindent The preposition “tré” takes a special form of this. It adds an –i- to the front of the 1<sup>st</sup>, 2<sup>nd</sup> and 5<sup>th</sup> form: –iem, –ieth, -ché, -chí, -ien, -sú, -chís:
%\noindent tréiem: across me
%\noindent tréieth: across you
%\noindent tréché: across him
%\noindent tréchí: across her
%\noindent tréien: across us
%\noindent trésú: across you (pl.)
%\noindent tréchís: across them
%<ol start="3">
%<li>Prepositions consisting only of one vowel</li>
%</ol>
%\noindent These are: a, e, u, i (to, from, of [with quantity], of [property])
%\noindent These take the following endings:
%\noindent -im, -ith, -é, -í, -in, -ú, -ís
%\noindent Also, the root form of the preposition changes:
%\noindent a &gt; adh-
%\noindent e &gt; ech-
%\noindent u &gt; uch-
%\noindent i &gt; ich-
%\noindent adhim: to me
%\noindent adhith: to you
%\noindent adhé: to him
%\noindent adhí: to her
%\noindent adhin: to us
%\noindent adhú: to you (pl.)
%\noindent adhís: to them
%<ol start="4">
%<li>Prepositions ending on -u</li>
%</ol>
%\noindent These are: au, didhíu (away from, outside)
%\noindent These prepositions don’t fuse with personal pronouns. The personal pronouns follow after them without changing. The object pronouns are used.
%\noindent au mi: away from me
%\noindent au ti: away from you
%\noindent au ché: away from him
%\noindent au chí: away from her
%\noindent au ni: away from us
%\noindent au sú: away from you (pl.)
%\noindent au chís: away from them
