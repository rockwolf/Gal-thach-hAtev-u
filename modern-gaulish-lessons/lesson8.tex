%<h2><span style="color:#ff0000;"><strong>Lesson 8: Adverbs</strong></span></h2>
%\noindent In the eighth lesson you will learn about adverbs.
%\noindent <strong>Adverbs</strong>
%\noindent An adverb is a word that describes a quality or a characteristic of a verbal action.
%\noindent English examples are: quickly, quietly, calmly, naturally, normally. In English they are usually formed with the suffix –ly.
%\noindent In Galáthach adverbs are formed by placing the particle “in” in front of an adjective. The adjective undergoes mutation of its initial consonant.
%\noindent áchu: quick
%\noindent in háchu: quickly
%\noindent áva mi chí: I do it
%\noindent &gt; áva mi chí in háchu: I do it quickly
%\noindent tau: quiet
%\noindent in dau: quietly
%\noindent spá í chí: she says it
%\noindent &gt; spá í chí in dau: she says it quietly
%\noindent aram: calm
%\noindent in haram: calm
%\noindent réna in avon: the river flows
%\noindent &gt; réna in avon in haram: the river flows calmly
%\noindent amvíthach: natural
%\noindent in hamvíthach: naturally
%\noindent gwóra cráré mel: bees produce honey
%\noindent &gt; gwóra cráré mel in hamvíthach
%\noindent suves: normal
%\noindent in shuves: normally
%\noindent né chwergha í co shé: she doesn’t act like that
%\noindent &gt; né chwergha í co shé in shuves
%\noindent <strong>Position of the adverb</strong>
%\noindent The adverb always follows the verb as closely as possible, after the subject and object of the phrase.
%\noindent Gwóra cráré mel in hamvíthach // “produce bees honey naturally”
%\noindent Verb   Sub. Obj.    Adverb               V          S     O          Adv
%\noindent <strong>Exercises</strong>
%\noindent Construct the adverbial form of the following adjectives. You can check your answers at the end of the lesson.
%\noindent cóil (narrow) &gt;
%\noindent lithan (wide) &gt;
%\noindent dianauch (poor) &gt;
%\noindent téithwár (wealthy) &gt;
%\noindent ardhu (high) &gt;
%\noindent íth (low) &gt;
%\noindent pethrarpenach (square) &gt;
%\noindent róthach (round) &gt;
%\noindent dái (good) &gt;

%\noindent <strong>Answers</strong>

%\noindent ardhu &gt; in hardhu (highly)
%\noindent íth &gt; in híth (lowly)
%\noindent pethrarpenach &gt; in betharpenach (squarely)
%\noindent róthach &gt; in rhothach (roundly)
%\noindent dái &gt; in dhái (well)
%\noindent druch &gt; in dhruch (badly)
